## About the project: What the client wanted.

The project as expected, involves delivering the complete(fullstack) web aspect of a multi-tenant freelance platform, while
also collaborating with an external mobile engineer to deliver the mobile app aspect of its front-end.

> The client's brief was to build a freelance platform that would connect freelancers with clients who need their services.
> The platform would be a one-stop shop for all things freelance - but targeted at only a specific niche market.
>
> Think [Upwork](https://www.upwork.com/) or [Fiverr](https://www.fiverr.com/), but for only one niche/domain and
> with some interesting additions. Some of the platform's features include:
>
> 1. Job listings + freelancer/client profiles - as expected.
> 2. Messaging(both text, audio, and likely video).
> 3. Payments.
> 4. Real-time location tracking - and more.

To make things more cool, we're building **custom implementations** for all those features - only making third-party
integrations where absolutely necessary.

Our understanding of what the client wanted was clear. Prior to us getting on board, the project already had quite some planning and
work in place, hence it was one we believed we could handle without much hassle.

## Tech Stack Used.

As usual, all our project at Zed Labs are built with state-of-the-art technologies. We're intentional about only giving
our clients the best services for their money. It means a lot that they entrusted us with delivering their projects.

The core project build consists of two stand-alone front-end clients with 3(multi-tenant) dashboards, and a microservices-based
backend.

### 1. The Front-end.

The project's front-ends are being built using [ReactJs](https://react.dev/) and [NextJs](https://nextjs.org/) with -
[TailwindCSS](https://tailwindcss.com/) for styling. This combination remains our
default choices for front-end development at Zed Labs. State management is currently being handled with [Redux-Toolkit](https://redux-toolkit.js.org/),
plus [Axios](https://axios-http.com/) and [SWR](https://swr.vercel.app/) for data fetching and revalidation.

[Headless UI](https://headlessui.com/) is our preferred UI Library. It's simple and perfect with just enough components to
help us fast-track UI development in the needed areas.

### 2. The Backend.

The Backend consists of multiple microservices being built with [Typescript](https://www.typescriptlang.org/)(NodeJs/ExpressJs)
and [Rust](https://www.rust-lang.org/).

### 3. Cloud/Infra and Deployments.

For cloud service, [AWS](https://aws.amazon.com/) remains our go-to choice at Zed Labs.

> I wrote an article on why we prefer to use AWS as our cloud service provider at Zed Labs. Check it out
> [here](/blog/categories/cloud-and-devops/why-we-prefer-aws-as-cloud-service-provider-at-zed-labs).

### 4. Payment Integration.

For payment integration, our choice has been [Paystack](https://paystack.com/). Just in case you don't know Paystack,
Paystack is a stripe company, that has been making massive strides in the African payment/fin-tech space.
